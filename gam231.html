During my Unreal Engine class, I was required to design and show a level to the entire class as part of the final project. For the project, since I liked nature, I wanted to design a world that looked like a serene countryside with a house at the end of the level. My discipline is normally to be an engineer for games, but I managed to pull through and create an interesting level with a fair amount of exploration. When the project was shown to the class, people saw how cool it was for me to pick a nature theme. Doing QA testing on the level was also very important, and I made sure that the player couldn't get stuck in certain areas, fall through the floor in the level or reach a state where they cannot win the level.


Insights

Even though I'm mainly involved in engineering games, I like to design levels too, and I want to wear many hats as someone who is seeking to be in the game industry. A common skill that many game developers have is to test games, which is one of the most important skills for ensuring a smooth, uninterrupted gameplay experience for customers. Testing the level over and over again was crucial in determining whether the game is too easy, too hard, or in the worst case, impossible.
